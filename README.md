A prototype test bench for wireless power transfer (WPT) systems, designed for research, development, and educational purposes. This setup enables the testing and validation of inductive power transfer technologies.

![WPT Setup](images/WPT_setup.png)

## Overview

Wireless Power Transfer (WPT) is a technology that enables the transmission of electrical energy from a power source to an electrical load without physical connections. This test bench provides a platform for:

- **Research & Development**: Testing new WPT topologies and control strategies
- **Performance Evaluation**: Measuring efficiency, power transfer capability, and coupling characteristics
- **Educational Purposes**: Demonstrating wireless power transfer principles and applications
- **Prototype Validation**: Verifying designs before production

## Features

- **Inductive Coupling**: Based on magnetic resonance or inductive coupling principles
- **Adjustable Parameters**: Configurable for different power levels and operating frequencies
- **Measurement Capabilities**: Integrated sensors for voltage, current, and power monitoring
- **Safety Features**: Built-in protection mechanisms for safe operation
- **Modular Design**: Flexible configuration for various experimental setups

## Components

The test bench typically includes:

- **Transmitter (Tx) Coil**: Primary coil that generates the magnetic field
- **Receiver (Rx) Coil**: Secondary coil that receives power from the magnetic field
- **Power Electronics**: Inverter/rectifier circuits for AC/DC conversion
- **Control System**: Microcontroller or DSP for system control and monitoring
- **Measurement Equipment**: Sensors and data acquisition for performance analysis
- **Load Bank**: Adjustable resistive/inductive loads for testing

Wireless power transfer technology has numerous applications including:
